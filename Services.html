Imaging Services

The MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging) machine uses powerful magnets and radio waves to create detailed images of your organs and tissues.

What to Expect: You'll lie still on a table that slides into the machine. The procedure is painless, but you might hear loud noises from the machine.

Benefits: MRI provides clear images without using radiation.

Risks: Some patients may feel claustrophobic inside the machine.

The CT (Computed Tomography) scan combines X-ray images taken from different angles and uses computer processing to create cross-sectional images.

What to Expect: You will lie on a table that slides into the CT scanner. The procedure is quick and painless.

Benefits: CT scans provide faster results and can help diagnose various conditions effectively.

Risks: There is a small amount of radiation exposure, but the benefits often outweigh the risks.

The PET (Positron Emission Tomography) CT scan combines metabolic imaging and anatomical imaging to provide comprehensive information about body functions.

What to Expect: You will receive a small injection of radioactive glucose, which helps highlight areas of concern during imaging.

Benefits: PET CT scans are valuable in detecting cancer and evaluating treatment response.

Risks: Minimal radiation exposure and potential allergic reactions to the radioactive material, though these are rare.

The X-Ray machine uses radiation to capture images of the inside of the body, primarily for viewing bones and other dense tissues.

What to Expect: You'll be positioned in front of the machine, and images will be taken quickly.

Benefits: X-Rays are widely used for quick diagnoses of fractures and other conditions.

Risks: Exposure to radiation, but it is minimal compared to the benefits of accurate diagnosis.

Treatment Services

The Linear Accelerator is used in radiation therapy to treat cancer by delivering high-energy radiation directly to tumors.

What to Expect: Treatments are non-invasive and painless, typically taking 15-30 minutes.

Benefits: High precision in targeting tumors while minimizing exposure to surrounding healthy tissues.

Risks: Mild side effects may occur, but serious complications are rare.

Mammography is a specialized X-ray used to examine breast tissue for early detection of breast cancer.

What to Expect: Your breast will be compressed between two plates to get clear images, which may cause slight discomfort.

Benefits: Early detection increases treatment success rates.

Risks: Minimal radiation exposure and discomfort from breast compression.

The Ultrasound machine uses high-frequency sound waves to create images of organs and structures inside the body.

What to Expect: A gel will be applied to your skin, and a transducer will be moved over the area being examined.

Benefits: Non-invasive and does not involve radiation.

Risks: Very few risks associated with ultrasound procedures.

The DNA Tester provides genetic testing to understand inherited conditions, ancestry, and other health-related information.

What to Expect: A small sample of saliva or blood will be taken for testing.

Benefits: Helps in early detection of genetic disorders.

Risks: Minimal, but potential emotional impact from results.

The Bone Mineral Density Checker assesses bone strength and risk of fractures using low-level X-ray technology.

What to Expect: You'll lie down on a table while a machine scans your bones.

Benefits: Early detection of osteoporosis.

Risks: Minimal radiation exposure.

Endoscopy involves using a flexible tube with a camera to visualize the inside of the digestive tract.

What to Expect: Sedation may be given, and the tube will be gently inserted through the mouth or rectum.

Benefits: Allows for both diagnosis and treatment of gastrointestinal issues.

Risks: Very minimal, but there is a small risk of bleeding or infection.

Cytology testing involves examining cells under a microscope to diagnose diseases, including cancer.

What to Expect: A small sample of cells will be taken from the body.

Benefits: Quick results that help in treatment planning.

Risks: Minimal risks associated with sample collection.

A biopsy involves removing a sample of tissue for laboratory testing to diagnose cancer or other diseases.

What to Expect: The procedure varies depending on the type of biopsy, but you may receive local anesthesia.

Benefits: Provides definitive diagnosis.

Risks: Potential for minor bleeding or infection.
