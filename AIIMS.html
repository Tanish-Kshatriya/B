The National Cancer Institute (NCI) at AIIMS is a world-class center dedicated to cutting-edge cancer treatment, groundbreaking research, and patient-centric care. Our goal is to eradicate cancer through innovation, awareness, and advanced medical expertise.

What is Cancer?

Cancer is the uncontrolled growth of abnormal cells in the body. It can affect any organ and spread rapidly if not treated.


There are over 100 types of cancer, each classified based on the affected organ or type of cell. Some cancers grow slowly, while others are highly aggressive. Understanding the nature of cancer helps in early detection and effective treatment.

Early Symptoms

Symptoms include unexplained weight loss, persistent fatigue, lumps or swelling, prolonged pain, and unusual bleeding.


Other warning signs may include chronic cough, difficulty swallowing, persistent indigestion, changes in bowel or bladder habits, skin changes, and non-healing sores. Early detection significantly improves survival rates.

Types of Cancer

Common types include lung cancer, breast cancer, leukemia, prostate cancer, and skin cancer.


Other prevalent cancers include colorectal cancer, pancreatic cancer, cervical cancer, liver cancer, and brain tumors. Each type has unique symptoms, risk factors, and treatment options.

Prevention Strategies

Adopting a healthy lifestyle, avoiding tobacco, maintaining a balanced diet, and undergoing regular screenings can reduce the risk of cancer.


Additional strategies include regular physical activity, maintaining a healthy weight, limiting alcohol consumption, protecting skin from excessive sun exposure, and getting vaccinated against cancer-related viruses such as HPV and Hepatitis B.

Treatment Options

Treatments include chemotherapy, radiation therapy, surgery, targeted therapy, and immunotherapy, depending on the type and stage of cancer.


Newer treatments include CAR-T cell therapy, hormone therapy, bone marrow transplantation, and proton therapy. Multidisciplinary approaches ensure personalized treatment plans tailored to each patient's needs.

Our ongoing research focuses on cancer genomics, immuno-oncology, bioinformatics, and nanotechnology-based drug delivery systems. We collaborate with international institutions to push the boundaries of cancer care.

Identifying whether a tumor is benign or malignant is crucial for treatment. Regular check-ups and imaging tests help in early detection and management.

Metastasis occurs when cancer cells spread from the primary site to other parts of the body through the bloodstream or lymphatic system.


Common metastatic sites include the lungs, liver, bones, and brain. Early detection of metastasis can significantly impact treatment success and patient outcomes.
